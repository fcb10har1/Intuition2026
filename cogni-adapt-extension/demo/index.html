Common Questions & Troubleshooting

Q: What if my PDF file is locked?

A: Use a PDF editor (Adobe, Preview, or free alternatives like PDFtk) to unlock your document before uploading. Locked files are automatically rejected by our submission validator. Instructions for each platform are available in the tech support guide. If your PDF was generated from a protected source (e.g., publisher ebook), contact IT support for alternative submission formats. Processing time for format exceptions is 2‚Äì3 business days.

Q: Can I submit late if I have documentation?

A: Late submissions with documentation are evaluated on a case-by-case basis. Student services documentation (medical, compassionate leave, etc.) must be submitted simultaneously with your request using the official form. Extensions of more than 7 days require approval from the dean's office. Late penalties apply: ‚Äì5% for each day late, capped at ‚Äì50% (zero grade if over cap). In exceptional cases, petitions can be submitted within 5 business days of the deadline for grade appeals.

Q: How is plagiarism checked?

A: All submissions are scanned through Turnitin before grading. Reports are available in your "Grade & Feedback" page within the portal. Similarity scores above 25% trigger manual review. Intentional plagiarism results in course failure and disciplinary action per university policy. Accidental duplication (e.g., reused phrasing from provided templates) is flagged but not penalized if properly cited. For clarification on specific passages, reply to your instructor's feedback comments within 10 days of grade publication.
